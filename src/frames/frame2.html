Formed in Birmingham, England, in 1969, Judas Priest is one of the pioneering bands of the heavy metal genre. Known for their twin-guitar attack, powerful vocals, and groundbreaking leather-and-studs image, the band helped define the sound and aesthetic of metal during the 1970s and 1980s. Judas Priest's music is characterized by its aggressive riffs, fast tempos, and Rob Halford’s operatic vocal style. Their 1980 album, British Steel, is often regarded as a classic, with songs like "Breaking the Law" and "Living After Midnight" becoming anthems for the genre.
