Manowar, an American heavy metal band formed in 1980, is renowned for its larger-than-life sound and unwavering dedication to the themes of fantasy, mythology, and epic battles. Founded by bassist Joey DeMaio and guitarist Ross the Boss in Auburn, New York, the band quickly carved out a niche with their bombastic style, self-proclaimed as "true metal." Their sound blends thunderous bass lines, shredding guitar solos, and powerful, operatic vocals, most notably delivered by frontman Eric Adams. Manowar's music is heavily influenced by classical compositions and the heroic epics of ancient mythology. Their lyrics often explore tales of warriors, gods, and glory, making their songs feel like soundtracks to mythical battles. Albums like Kings of Metal (1988) and Warriors of the World (2002) became iconic in the metal community, with anthems such as "Hail and Kill" and "Warriors of the World United" embodying their grandiose, martial approach to metal.
